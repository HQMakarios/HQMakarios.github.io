    【Urban Design】Planning and design of historic area along the river in Tianjin

    heqian
    2019-06-30
    blog

    Location: Three forks Estuary, Tianjin city
    Site area:18ha
    Type: Historic Location Planning and Design
    Individual work

    In ancient times, there was the Three forks Estuary, and later came the city of Tianjin, with the base situated at the origin of Tianjin – the confluence of the Trifurcation River. The area surrounding the base is rich in historical relics and has a deep cultural heritage.

    The base has witnessed the development of the city of Tianjin and was the cradle of Tianjin’s urban culture and economy. However, in recent times, it has gradually declined.

    To reawaken economic vitality, redefine cultural uniqueness, enhance the quality of life, and ensure the sustainable development of the area, a comprehensive planning and design approach is taken from cultural, landscape, and economic perspectives.

    Site analysis

    History

    Resources & Issues

    Within the base, there are three main elements: the South Grand Canal of Tianjin, a 600-year-old Estimation Clothing Street, and the former bustling market area (Big Hutong Commercial City) that used to be in the hearts of old Tianjin residents.
    However, as history progressed, rising water levels led to the deterioration of the riverbanks, Estimation Clothing Street lost its maintenance and gradually declined, and the commercial district’s single model has become obsolete

    Proposal & Master plan

    Based on the analysis of the current status of the site and the needs of the population, a summary of issues and design strategies for three areas in need of improvement is provided. Specific design strategies are employed for each distinct area, maximizing the expression of their unique characteristics.

    Cultural sustainability (Step1)

    Due to the demolition and damage to historical buildings in the old street, the layout of streets and alleys has been disrupted, and the traditional appearance of the old city is gradually disappearing. In order to protect cultural heritage and preserve the urban memories of the citizens, the design focuses on three aspects to continue the historical heritage.

    Cultural sustainability (Step2)

    As old and time-honored shops gradually declined and folk cultural activities dwindled, in order to meet the daily shopping needs and folk culture experiential demands of the citizens, revitalization was sparked through methods such as updating the functions of traditional shops. This was achieved by incorporating creativity and reintegrating vendors, among other specific activity planning, to rekindle the bustling street scenes.

    Ecological sustainability

    The design plot is located adjacent to the South Canal and lacks greenery and waterfront landscapes within the plot. The ecological aspects of the riverbank have been compromised. To provide a pleasant and eco-friendly residential and recreational environment, the first step is to increase green spaces and public facilities. Additionally, during the rainy season when the river water rises and floods the riverbank, to ensure internal safety, a sustainably utilized water storage pool is designed, which can also serve as an activity square during dry periods.

    Financial Sustainability

    Based on the calculation of future economic development for the plot, it has been determined that additional apartments are required to attract talent.
