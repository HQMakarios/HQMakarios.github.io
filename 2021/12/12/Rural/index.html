    Location: Location: Sufang Town, Pucheng county,Shaanxi Province
    Site area:1ha
    Time:2021.12-2022.1
    Type: Rural planning and design
    Group work

    Dangding Village, a migrant village, has transformed over time from a collective settlement to a scattered pattern, integrated with farmland across the land. Changes in time and space inspire us. As Lewis Carroll once said, ‘A blank navigational chart is always the most captivating; it is the joy of exploration itself.’

    Su Fang Town in 2050 is indeed such a blank chart. We know it must encompass part of Dangding Village within, but once you set foot on this land, no one can confidently define Dangding Village’s exact boundary. The village is like a puzzle, an enigmatic entity, embracing the drifting sea of information. Every effort to understand Dangding Village is an act of exploration, a stroke on the blank canvas.

    History of rural development

    Concept formation-（Analysis of rural characteristics in 2050）

    By 2050, land operation and transfer have become trading mechanisms, enabling unimpeded resource circulation between urban and rural areas. Full-scale mechanized agricultural production is in place, with a declining rural population, making it possible to monitor the production process remotely. The urbanization process steadily accelerates.

    Village driving mechanisms

    In 2050, Dangding Village has inevitably undergone significant social transformation, exhibiting a fluid state during this phase. As fundamental elements such as productivity and production relations continuously evolve, various surface-level elements will change accordingly. The initial research categorizes rural elements into three parts: productive elements, sustaining elements, and controlling elements.

    The cycle formed by the participation of productive elements is the essence of rural existence; the sustaining elements supplement the productive elements and assist the composition of the cycle; the controlling elements maintain the continuous operation of the cycle.And these three elements are the drivers of village development, with the changing relationships and roles between them shaping the village of 2050.
